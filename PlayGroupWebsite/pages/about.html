Where To Find Us

The playgroup is located in the East Craigs Church Hall, which is behind the Dentist and Doctors' Surgeries in Bughtlin Market. (See Contact for full address and map).

Our Activities

The morning’s activities are mostly centred around free play, we always have a variety of toys on offer to help your child learn through play. For example we have a home corner, a reading corner, jigsaws, playdough, water and sand play, painting/gluing, and construction toys. During this time children are able to stop for a drink (either water or milk) and a snack which always includes fruit.

Session Times

Sessions run during school term times Monday to Friday mornings from 9.15am to 11.15am.

Cost

Daily sessions costs £6 per child, which is payable on a per term basis at the start of each term. We also charge a non-refunable administration fee of £10 when you child starts then per academic year thereafter. Children can be placed on our waiting list from 1 year old and on reaching 2½ will be invited into the playgroup for a morning's taster visit

The playgroup aims to provide a safe fun environment for your children to experience many types of play, develop skills and make friends. We have a dedicated staff who are all experienced in working with children and value each child as an individual.

Our Beliefs

We believe in learning through play and our activities are centred on making learning fun. We look to giving the children opportunities to progress in each of the following areas of learning and development:

  • Personal, social and emotional development: we want to help children develop self-confidence and learn how to positively interact with their peers
  • Physical development: we provide opportunities for the children to develop their co-ordination and movement skills
  • Communication development

Through play the children can develop their

  • Literacy skills – we have a range of books on offer in our reading corner that the children can enjoy by themselves or with a member of staff
  • Maths and science skills – through play the children and staff talk about sizes, numbers, shapes and counting
  • Understanding the world – we observe the physical world looking at different seasons
  • Expressive arts and design: children are able to express themselves creatively through painting and modelling and at the music corner
